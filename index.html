Getting Started

Welcome to macapture

macapture is a screen capture utility for macOS that lets you take screenshots, record your screen, and annotate captures with ease. Follow the steps below to get up and running quickly.

  1. Install macapture from the Mac App Store and launch it from your Applications folder.
  2. Grant permissions — when prompted, allow Screen Recording access in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Screen Recording.
  3. Set up a shortcut — open macapture Preferences to configure your preferred keyboard shortcut for capturing.
  4. Take your first capture — press your shortcut (default: ⌘⇧5) and select an area, window, or full screen.

Frequently Asked Questions

You can capture your screen using the keyboard shortcut configured in macapture Preferences. By default, pressing ⌘⇧5 opens the capture toolbar where you can choose to capture a selection, window, or the full screen.

You can also click the macapture icon in the menu bar to start a capture at any time.

By default, screenshots are saved to your Desktop. You can change the save location in macapture Preferences → General → Save Location. Supported destinations include Desktop, Documents, Downloads, or any custom folder you choose.

macapture supports saving screenshots as PNG, JPEG, TIFF, PDF, and WebP. Screen recordings are saved as MP4 (H.264) or MOV. You can set your preferred format in Preferences → Format.

If the macapture icon is missing from the menu bar, try the following steps:

  1. Open Finder → Applications and double-click macapture to relaunch it.
  2. Check macapture Preferences → General and ensure "Show in Menu Bar" is enabled.
  3. If your menu bar is crowded, hold ⌘ and drag icons to rearrange or reveal hidden ones.
  4. Restart your Mac if the issue persists.

When starting a screen recording, click the Options button in the capture toolbar and select your microphone from the Microphone dropdown. To record internal system audio, ensure you have granted macapture the necessary audio permissions in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone.

After taking a screenshot, macapture shows a thumbnail preview in the corner of your screen. Click the thumbnail to open the annotation editor, where you can add arrows, text, shapes, highlights, and more. Changes are saved automatically when you close the editor.

Troubleshooting

Screen Recording Permission Denied

If macapture can't capture your screen, you may need to grant Screen Recording permission:

  1. Open System Settings (Apple menu → System Settings).
  2. Navigate to Privacy & Security → Screen & System Audio Recording.
  3. Enable the toggle next to macapture.
  4. Relaunch macapture when prompted.

Screenshots Are Blank or Black

Blank captures are usually caused by missing permissions or GPU-accelerated content protection. Try these steps:

  • Verify Screen Recording permission is granted (see above).
  • Quit and relaunch macapture.
  • If the blank capture is from a DRM-protected app (e.g. streaming video), content protection prevents capturing — this is expected behavior.
  • Update macapture to the latest version via the Mac App Store.

Keyboard Shortcut Doesn't Work

Another app may be using the same shortcut. To fix this:

  1. Open macapture Preferences → Shortcuts.
  2. Click the current shortcut and type a new key combination.
  3. Alternatively, check System Settings → Keyboard → Keyboard Shortcuts for conflicts.
